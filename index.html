1) Executive Summary

This offer is designed for an investor or organization seeking to deploy a scalable electric taxi fleet in Rwanda through a structured operating partnership with KivuRide. The proposal provides two alternatives: (A) Full cash acquisition and (B) NCBA-supported financing (100% principal financing structure with a refundable 10% deposit under the program mechanics).

Two Models

  • Model A: Cash acquisition (highest margin, simplest structure).
  • Model B: NCBA financing via an operating company + qualified drivers.

Supplier & Support

  • Vehicles supplied and supported by Long Tai Automobile International Ltd.
  • Official distribution ecosystem (garage, spare parts, after-sales readiness).
  • 5-year warranty coverage via YNAMMI 06 warranty card issued through authorized channels.

KivuRide Operations

  • Demand generation + dispatch via mobile app (two-sided platform).
  • Call center & customer support.
  • Driver management + reporting & owner dashboard visibility.

2) Why This Opportunity Exists Now

Rwanda has made e-mobility a national priority and continues to adopt incentives and reforms that reduce barriers to electric vehicle uptake, including fiscal incentives and enabling infrastructure policy. This creates a favorable environment for early fleet deployment in Kigali.

Policy Momentum for E-Mobility

  • National direction toward climate-resilient transport and e-mobility incentives.
  • Reducing EV adoption barriers through tax and infrastructure measures (e.g., charging ecosystem support).

See References: Government climate transport updates and incentives summaries.

Fleet Renewal Pressure in Commercial Transport

  • Rwanda’s transport regulator (RURA) sets and enforces compliance for road transport operations, including taxi cab operator standards.
  • Public transport regulations include vehicle age-limit provisions (Official Gazette instrument available via RURA).

This proposal aligns with the direction of fleet modernization by deploying new, warranty-backed electric vehicles designed for the region.

5) Two Acquisition Models (Choose One)

Model A — Full Cash Payment

Simple structure • Highest margin

Best for investors seeking immediate ownership and simplified execution without bank repayment obligations.

  • Direct acquisition from authorized supplier (Long Tai)
  • Assets can be owned personally or under a company
  • Operations executed under KivuRide (drivers + monitoring)

Operating Cost Structure (Per Vehicle)

  • App + Call Center + Internet: 100,000 FRW / month
  • Management Fee: 30,000 FRW / month
  • Charging: 18,000 FRW / week (≈ 72,000 FRW / month)
  • Insurance: 1,400,000 FRW / year (≈ 116,667 FRW / month)

Model B — NCBA Financing (Driver-Support Structure)

Scale with financing • Program-aligned

Built for scaling through a financing program structure aligned with driver participation: the investor forms a company, onboards eligible drivers, and submits required documents through the company.

  • Loan principal: 100% financing structure
  • Deposit: 10% deposit submitted, refundable upon vehicle arrival + yellow card issuance (program mechanism)
  • Term: 5 years • Interest: 15% APR (compounded monthly)
  • Collateral: Vehicle serves as collateral under program narrative

Required Execution Structure

  • Investor establishes a company for fleet operation
  • Company onboards licensed taxi drivers with operating statements
  • Driver documents + company documents submitted under NCBA requirements
  • Company receives financing approval; fleet registered under company
  • KivuRide supports onboarding and operationalization
Decision Point: Choose Model A (Cash) or Model B (NCBA Financing). Both models operate under the same KivuRide ecosystem.

9) Compliance & Governance (RURA Alignment)

Rwanda Utilities Regulatory Authority (RURA) regulates road transport service standards and ensures taxi cab operators comply with applicable rules and licensing requirements. This proposal is structured to support compliant fleet deployment through documented ownership, driver qualification, and operational traceability.

Operational Compliance (Practical)

  • Operate through a formal ownership structure (individual or company)
  • Onboard drivers with appropriate licensing and documentation
  • Maintain required insurance coverage
  • Ensure vehicles are registered and compliant with taxi cab standards

Governance & Transparency (Owner Protection)

  • Owner dashboard access for revenue and utilization monitoring
  • Call center logging and trip traceability
  • Driver performance metrics and accountability
  • Structured monthly reporting and operational oversight
